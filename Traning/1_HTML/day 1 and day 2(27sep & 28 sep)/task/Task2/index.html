Welcome to Vishwas Nangare Patil's Biography

Vishwas Nangare Patil

Director general of the Anti Corruption Bureau, Maharashtra

Vishwas Narayan Nangare Patil

Vishwas Nangare Patil is a well-known Indian Police Service (IPS) officer from Maharashtra, celebrated for his bravery, leadership, and modern approach to law enforcement. He gained nationwide recognition for his courageous role during the 26/11 Mumbai terrorist attacks, where he played a crucial part in containing the attackers and saving lives. His actions during this critical time earned him the prestigious President’s Police Medal for Gallantry.

He is also an advocate of community policing, where the police and public work together to maintain law and order. He has been a strong supporter of using technology to modernize policing and combat emerging threats like cybercrime and terrorism.

Apart from his policing work, Nangare Patil is a motivational speaker and an author. His book, "Man Me Hai Vishwas," shares his life journey and offers inspiration to the youth. He remains a prominent figure, known for his leadership and vision in the Maharashtra Police Department, and continues to inspire those aspiring to serve society through public service.

Early Life and Education

  • Born in the Sangli district of Maharashtra.
  • Grew up in a humble family with strong values of discipline and service.
  • Developed an early interest in public service and aimed to become a police officer.
  • Cleared the highly competitive UPSC Civil Services Examination.
  • Trained at the Lal Bahadur Shastri National Academy of Administration,India's premier civil service institute.

    • Career Milestones

  • Joined the Indian Police Service (IPS) and began serving in the Maharashtra Police Department.
  • Gained recognition during the 26/11 Mumbai terrorist attacks in 2008 as the Deputy Commissioner of Police (DCP) for the Western Zone.
  • Played a pivotal role in containing the terrorist attacks, saving lives, and maintaining public order.
  • Awarded the President’s Police Medal for Gallantry for his exceptional bravery during the attacks.

    • Achievements

  • Successfully handled the 26/11 Mumbai terror attacks in 2008.
  • Became the youngest IPS officer to be appointed as the Special Inspector General of Police, Nashik Range.
  • Worked extensively in tackling organized crime and naxalism in Maharashtra.
  • Instrumental in reducing crime rates significantly in the regions under his jurisdiction.

    • Awards

  • President's Police Medal for Gallantry in 2015 for his bravery during the 26/11 attacks.
  • Maharashtra State's Best Police Officer Award in 2010.
  • Police Excellence Award for his contributions to law enforcement and public safety.
  • Best Anti-Terror Operations Award by the Government of Maharashtra.
