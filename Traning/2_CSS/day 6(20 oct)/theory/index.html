After the demise of the legendary Mr. Chandrakant Sampat earlier this year, India lost another of her value investing legends, Mr. Parag Parikh, on 3rd May 2015. Mr. Parikh died in a car accident in Omaha, US, while on his way to the airport after attending the annual shareholders’ meeting of Warren Buffett’s Berkshire Hathaway. In hindsight, this sounds eerie as Mr. Parikh, a true value investor, breathed his last in the place that’s called the Mecca for value investors. It was in 2012 when I first saw Mr. Parag Parikh at a conference. Knowing him to be a veteran of the Indian value investing industry, I had imagined him to be an old, serious man. But he was anything but old and serious. We were meeting for the first time, but it didn’t seem that way, given how he made me comfortable in his company. Partly it was his limitless energy and unpretentious, floodlight smile that made it sound as if he was very happy to meet people and share his wisdom. But he also resembled strongly a child who had dreams of changing the world – the fund management industry in his case – for the better.

Ordered List position

  1. Leadership : Leadership is the ability to guide, influence, and inspire others to work towards a common goal or vision. Effective leaders provide direction, build a positive team culture, and empower their team members to reach their full potential. Leadership is not just about authority or management—it’s about motivating and supporting others while navigating challenges with confidence and clarity.
  2. Adaptable:Adaptability is the ability to adjust to new conditions, challenges, or environments quickly and effectively. Leaders who are adaptable can thrive in situations of uncertainty and change, responding flexibly to new information or circumstances. They are open to learning, unafraid of shifting strategies, and can pivot when necessary. This skill helps maintain team momentum, even when things don't go as planned.
  3. Good Listner: Being a good listener is crucial for strong leadership. It involves actively hearing and understanding others' perspectives, concerns, and ideas without interrupting or making assumptions. Good listeners foster open communication, build trust, and create a supportive environment where team members feel heard and valued. Leaders with this skill are better equipped to make informed decisions, resolve conflicts, and strengthen relationships within the team.
  4. Quick Learner A quick learner is someone who can rapidly acquire new knowledge, skills, or understanding. Leaders who are quick learners can absorb and apply information efficiently, allowing them to keep up with evolving trends, technologies, or business practices. This skill is essential in fast-paced environments, where the ability to learn and adapt quickly ensures that leaders can make timely decisions, stay competitive, and lead their teams effectively through change.
